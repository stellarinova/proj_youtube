How to Get Your Spiritual Fire Back When You Feel Distant from God

Friend, do you feel dry, how about depleted? Or maybe you feel like somewhere along the way you've gone from burning for Jesus, to lukewarm, and you're not even sure when it happened.

Many believers find themselves in a familiar yet troubling place: once passionate about their faith, they now feel spiritually dry, depleted, or merely going through religious motions without genuine connection. Dr. Jamie Morgan, with over three decades in ministry, acknowledges this common struggle and offers hope—along with practical steps—to rekindle that lost spiritual fire.

Identify What Smothered Your Faith

The first step to recovery is honest diagnosis. You can't fix what you haven't named. Before taking any action, get honest with God about how you arrived at this spiritually dry place. Was it busyness that crowded out intimacy? Was it unaddressed sin creating distance? Was it battle weariness from prolonged spiritual conflict? Or perhaps distraction from good things that gradually replaced God as your center?

Ask God directly to reveal the true condition of your heart. He will faithfully show you—not to condemn, but to heal. Don't run from what He reveals or make excuses. Honesty before God is the essential first step to getting your fire back, because you cannot address what you refuse to acknowledge.

Repent and Return to Your First Love

Drawing from Jesus' message to the church in Ephesus (Revelation 2:4), Dr. Morgan reminds us that spiritual drift often looks like continued activity without genuine affection. The Ephesians were still working, serving, and showing up—but their first love had faded. Repentance isn't about beating yourself up; it's simply about turning around.

True repentance says: "Lord, I got off track. I chose things over You, and I'm coming back." The beautiful promise is that God isn't standing with arms crossed waiting for you to grovel—He's running toward you with open arms, just like the father of the prodigal son. All He needs is your willingness to turn around.

Get Back Into the Word and Prayer with Intention

Spiritual renewal begins with intentional engagement in God's Word and prayer—not checking off religious boxes or squeezing in quiet time between appointments, but purposefully seeking Jesus. Dr. Morgan shares how a relentless Christian bookstore owner's challenge to "read the book of Acts with fresh eyes" eventually sparked a desperation for more of Jesus that transformed his spiritual life.

That hunger for God always begins with the Word. When you open your Bible and invite the Holy Spirit to speak through it with fresh eyes, the Word ignites a fire that leads to deeper hunger for Jesus. This isn't about striving or trying harder—it's about cultivating raw, genuine hunger for God's presence.

Ask God for a Fresh Infilling of His Spirit

Perhaps the most transformative step is requesting a fresh infilling of the Holy Spirit. Contrary to common misconceptions, the Holy Spirit is not an introverted silent partner in the Trinity—He is the all-consuming fire of God living inside believers. The same fire that descended on the 120 believers at Pentecost—powerful enough to win the world for Christ—is still available today.

Dr. Morgan testifies that after praying for everything God had for him, he experienced a life-changing encounter where spiritual light and heat flooded his being. His spiritual engine went from operating on just two cylinders to all eight. This same Holy Spirit fire is waiting for anyone who will openly ask: "Lord, I want everything You have for me. I'm done with lukewarm Christian living. I want Your fire—all of it."

Stir It Up Daily: Yield, Feed, and Protect the Fire

Receiving the fire is one thing; keeping it burning requires daily tending. Based on Paul's instruction to Timothy (2 Timothy 1:6) to "fan into flame the gift of God," believers have a three-fold responsibility: yield to the Spirit daily, stir up the fire through spirit-led prayer and worship, and stay in the Word with hunger.

Crucially, we must never quench the Spirit (1 Thessalonians 5:19) by returning to the distractions, busyness, or noise that originally smothered our fire. God's fire within us is not self-sustaining—it requires our daily "yes," choosing His presence over competing attentions, and maintaining spiritual hunger and yieldedness.

As Dr. Morgan concludes with his activation prayer, believers can receive this fresh infilling by faith: asking for God's consuming fire, requesting to be saturated with the Holy Spirit, and committing to be empowered witnesses who set the world ablaze with the gospel wherever they go.
